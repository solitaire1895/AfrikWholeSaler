1. Executive Summary
AfrikWholesaler is repositioned from a multi-vendor marketplace into a single-company B2B sourcing and
wholesale platform. AfrikWholesaler is the only seller of record: it sources from Chinese manufacturers, negotiates
pricing, performs quality control, manages logistics, and delivers to customers across Africa. Factories are never
exposed to customers, and every supplier-facing feature (registration, dashboards, ratings, messaging, verification) is
removed from scope entirely.
This plan translates that business model into a build sequence: what to build first, what can wait, and where a small
number of well-chosen innovations would meaningfully improve conversion, trust, and operational efficiency without
expanding scope. Ratings follow a MoSCoW-style scale: Must-Have (blocks launch), Should-Have (launch is fine
without it, but weak without it for long), and Good-to-Have (genuine improvement, safe to defer).

6. Step-by-Step Development Roadmap
Phase 0 — Foundations (Week 1)
● Finalize data model (products, customers, orders, quotes, shipments, chat, RBAC)
● Set up PostgreSQL with Row-Level Security policies per role/customer
● Provision Supabase project, storage buckets, and auth
● Set up Next.js monorepo: public app + internal admin route group
● CI/CD pipeline, staging environment, error monitoring (Sentry)

Phase 1 — Core Commerce (Weeks 2–3)
● Product catalog CRUD + price tiers + badges (admin side)
● Public catalog browsing, product detail page, search/filter
● Customer registration/login, profile, addresses, documents
● Orders, invoices, and payment integration (Stripe/Paystack/Flutterwave)
● Shipping & import cost calculators on product pages

Phase 2 — Sourcing & Communication (Weeks 4–5)
● Request a Quote flow (customer form → internal quote queue → staff response)
● Live chat backbone: Socket.IO server, conversation model, agent assignment
● Internal staff notes, tags, priority levels on conversations
● File/image upload in chat and quote attachments
● Notification pipeline: email + push for chat and order events

Phase 3 — AI Layer (Week 6)
● AI sourcing assistant: intent parsing → catalog recommendation
● AI-assisted shipping/customs estimate generation
● AI chatbot as first responder in live chat with human handoff rules
● Guardrails: AI never quotes final binding price without staff confirmation

Phase 4 — Operations & Logistics (Week 7)
● Warehouse & inventory management screens
● Internal Purchase Requests module (bridges customer demand to real factory sourcing)
● Logistics/shipment tracking, carrier status updates, customs documents
● Operations Manager reporting views

Phase 5 — Admin, Roles & Content (Week 8)
● Roles & Permissions matrix UI for Super Admin

● Employee management (invite, deactivate, role assignment)
● Content management for landing page sections and articles
● Discounts & basic marketing tools

Phase 6 — Hardening & Launch Readiness (Weeks 9–10)
● Security review: RLS policy audit, auth flow pen-test, rate limiting
● Load test chat and checkout flows
● Accessibility and mobile QA across customer and admin apps
● Backup/restore drill, incident runbook, on-call rotation
● Soft launch with a limited customer cohort before full rollout

8. Risk & Compliance Notes
●

Customs/tax estimates are estimates only — display clear disclaimers; actual duties vary by destination
country and are confirmed at the quote stage by staff, not automatically bound by the AI assistant.

●

AI assistant scope limits — the AI should recommend and estimate, but final binding price and delivery
commitments must be confirmed by a human sales representative.

●

Document handling — customer documents (IDs, business registration, customs paperwork) should be stored
in access-controlled buckets with signed URLs, not public storage.

●

Internal staff notes — must be filtered server-side by role; never trust a frontend flag alone to hide sensitive
internal commentary from customers.

●

Payment flows — for high-value B2B orders, consider milestone-based payment release (deposit → balance on
shipment) rather than single full-payment capture.
